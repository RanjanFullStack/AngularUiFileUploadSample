Fund Terms
Vintage Year:
A year in which the first influx of capital is delivered to a PE fund by investors
Target Commitment:
The total capital commitment expected to be received by a PE fund
Maximum Commitment:
The capital commitment beyond which further investors will not be admitted
Fund Size:
Total capital raised by a fund
GP Commitment:
Capital committed by General Partner as a percentage of total capital commitment
Preferred Return Percent:
Threshold return that limited partners of a private equity fund must receive, prior to the general partners receiving its carried interest
GP Catchup Percent:
Catchup is the percentage / rate at which GP starts receiving its carry after the preferred return distribution to LP
Management Fee:
Fee charged by fund manager for managing the fund
Management Fee Offset:
The extent to which monitoring, transaction and other portfolio company related expenses paid to the General Partner are offset against management fees
Fund Term:
Duration of a private equity fund before complete dilution
Maximum Extension To Fund Term:
Number of years by which fund term of a fund can be increased with the consent of LPs
Fund Closing Date:
Fund closing refers to a date by which investors must have legally committed to investing in the fund
Org Expenses:
Expenses related to establishing and organizing a fund and its infrastructure
Clawback:
Refers to the limited partners' right to reclaim part of the general partners' carried interest, where general partners received excess compensation
